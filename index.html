Who are we?

It started off with me being late class back in Highschool where the travel time was very long and traffic, atrocious even by car. Better yet, sometimes I would have to beg my teacher to see if I can leave the class early just so that I can make it to the bus to get home before it leaves 10 minutes after school ended.

Being late was still a thing a thing when I was in university. Either it was the busses no coming or canceled or traffic, or sometimes even both. I've nearly had enough of being late several times a week, it was just tiring to deal with.

But when portals and teleportation was discovered, everything for me changed. Knowing that I was top in the field of quantum tunnels in my university class, I knew I had to do something. After finishing my masters weeks after the discovery I immediately applied to the company that discovered it. I was accepted in. A few years have gone by, learnt more things then I could ever dream of. An opportunity arose when the company wanted to create a subsidiary focusing on travel and how this new technology could be utilized. With years under my belt, I was appointed head.

And that's how port was created

Now port is in the small testing phase or our project and ironing things out. We currently have around a few hundred beta tester including our staff member located in Auckland New Zealand helping us improve our service.

More is yet to come from Port and to everyone else.

Our Values

Progression:

We strive for innovation and progression to provide a better world to life in for everything from plants - Humans and anything in-between.

Integrity:

We believe doing things the right way and hold accountability for everything that we do.

Honestly/Transparency:

We hold ourselves to a very high standard as we could stand at the forefront of everyone's eyes and having trust with our customer is core to us.

Reliability:

As a transport service, we need to be available 24/7 as transport is one of the most important systems to get us from A to B and we cant afford to be down even for a second.
